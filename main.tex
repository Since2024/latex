\chapter*{ABSTRACT}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{ABSTRACT}

Government offices in Nepal face significant challenges due to manual form-filling processes that are time-consuming, error-prone, and inefficient. This project presents the development of an Auto Form Fill-Up Application designed to automate the extraction and filling of government forms, specifically the Land and Property Tax Form (Sampati Kar) and Business Tax Registration Form. 

The system utilizes Google Gemini Vision API for intelligent document understanding and Tesseract OCR as a fallback mechanism, enabling robust data extraction from citizenship certificates and business documents. Implemented using Python 3.11 with Streamlit for the user interface, the application features template-based form mapping, data validation, user profile management, and PDF generation with proper Devanagari font rendering.

Testing with real government documents demonstrated 85-90\% extraction accuracy with Gemini AI and 70-75\% with OCR fallback. The system reduces form processing time from 15-30 minutes to 3-5 minutes per citizen, representing a 70-80\% efficiency improvement. The application successfully demonstrates the feasibility of modernizing government services through practical technology implementation while requiring minimal infrastructure and technical expertise.

\chapter{INTRODUCTION}

\section{Introduction}
In Nepal, official systematic procedures truly test our patience, 
particularly when it comes to paperwork. Every sector government 
offices, banks, administrative bodies, and educational institutions
requires extensive application procedures and form filling as part 
of their workflow. Even in this modern technological era, we continue 
to rely on manual form-filling processes. These forms are essential 
for citizen services, legal documentation, and public record maintenance.
Manual form filling is time-consuming, labor-intensive, and highly 
prone to errors such as mistyping and data omission. During my 
observations at municipal offices, I noticed a critical insight: 
the information required to fill most government forms is already 
available in citizens' official documents national identity cards, 
citizenship certificates, and business registration papers. The same 
personal details are entered repeatedly across different forms, 
creating unnecessary redundancy and frustration.

This observation led to the development of the Auto Form Fill-Up 
Application. The system addresses this inefficiency by automatically 
extracting data from legal documents and certificates, then intelligently 
mapping this information to the appropriate fields in government forms. 
By harnessing technologies such as Optical Character Recognition (OCR), 
Google Gemini AI for semantic understanding, PDF generation tools, and 
database management systems, the application modernizes form handling 
for Nepal's public sector.

The Auto Form Fill-Up App is designed to support mass processing of 
forms, generate accurately filled documents in PDF format, and provide 
a user-friendly interface suitable for government office staff with 
minimal technical expertise. The application serves as a viable prototype 
demonstrating how Nepal's bureaucratic processes can be made more 
efficient, secure, and transparent through technology. By automating 
repetitive data entry tasks, the system enhances workflow efficiency, 
improves public service experience, and paves a new path toward 
digitalization of government operations.


\section{Problem Statement}
Government offices in Nepal, particularly sub-metropolitan municipalities, face 
numerous challenges due to their continued reliance on manual form-filling 
processes. This project focuses on two critical forms commonly processed at 
sub-metropolitan offices: the Land and Property Tax Form (Sampati Kar) and the 
Business Tax Registration Form. These forms represent high-volume, repetitive 
tasks that exemplify the broader challenges of manual bureaucratic processes.

During an internship at a municipal office, several critical inefficiencies were 
identified in the current manual system. Citizens arriving with their citizenship 
certificates, business registration documents, naksha pass certificates, and other 
official papers must wait in long queues while office staff manually transcribe 
information into multiple forms. Due to limited staffing, sub-metropolitan offices 
often hire students from different campuses for internships to manage the workload. 
The same personal details—name, father's name, address, citizenship number—are 
copied repeatedly across different documents, consuming significant time and 
resources. This repetitive manual process creates multiple points of failure and 
frustration for both citizens and government employees.

The following key problems were observed in the manual form-filling system:

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbullet, leftmargin=*, nosep, itemsep=3pt, topsep=2pt]
 \item \textbf{High Error Rate:} Human data entry is inherently susceptible to 
   spelling mistakes, numerical transposition errors, field omissions, and 
   inconsistencies between documents. Even minor errors in critical fields such 
   as citizenship numbers or property identification can lead to legal complications 
   and require time-consuming corrections.

 \item \textbf{Time-Intensive Manual Processing:} During peak periods such as tax 
   filing deadlines, bulk form submissions create extensive queues. Office staff 
   must manually extract data from source documents, transcribe information into 
   multiple forms, cross-check entries for accuracy, and file physical copies—a 
   process that can take 15-30 minutes per citizen, resulting in severe productivity 
   losses.

 \item \textbf{Lack of Intelligent Data Extraction:} Current systems lack 
   automated mechanisms to read and interpret information from official documents. 
   Staff must visually scan citizenship certificates and business papers, then 
   manually type each field, making the process labor-intensive and prone to 
   transcription errors.

 \item \textbf{Absence of Reusable Data Templates:} Each form type requires 
   separate manual effort with no standardized digital templates. The lack of 
   structured configuration systems means adding new form types requires significant 
   development effort rather than simple template creation.

 \item \textbf{Fragmented Data Storage:} Paper-based forms and isolated digital 
   records scattered across different registers make data storage, retrieval, and 
   updates extremely difficult. There is no centralized database system enabling 
   efficient record management, historical tracking, or cross-form data reusability.

 \item \textbf{Incompatible PDF Generation:} When digital forms are generated, 
   they often fail to properly render Nepali Devanagari script or match official 
   government template formats, resulting in rejected submissions and requiring 
   manual recreation of forms.

 \item \textbf{Redundant Data Entry for Repeat Users:} Citizens who require 
   multiple government services must provide identical information repeatedly. 
   The absence of user profile systems means no mechanism exists to save and 
   reuse verified citizen data across different forms and services, creating 
   unnecessary frustration and workload.

 \item \textbf{Limited Scalability:} The manual system cannot efficiently handle 
   volume fluctuations. During peak periods such as fiscal year-end, offices become 
   overwhelmed, leading to extended wait times, rushed processing, and increased 
   error rates, with no viable solution for scaling operations.
\end{enumerate}

These interconnected issues create significant bottlenecks that negatively impact 
both government operational efficiency and citizen satisfaction. Manual processes 
consume valuable staff time that could be better spent on service delivery and 
decision-making. Citizens experience frustration due to long wait times, repeated 
data entry requirements, and processing delays. From a broader perspective, these 
inefficiencies undermine public trust in government services and hinder Nepal's 
progress toward digital governance. 

To address these challenges, there is a critical need for a comprehensive digital 
solution that automates form filling through intelligent data extraction, maintains 
a centralized database for efficient record management, validates data to ensure 
accuracy, improves processing turnaround time, and enables data reusability—all 
while minimizing disruption to current workflows and requiring minimal technical 
expertise from office staff.

\section{Scope and Limitations}
This Auto Form Fill-Up Application is designed specifically for automating two commonly used government forms at sub-metropolitan offices in Nepal: the Land and Property Tax Form (Sampati Kar) and the Business Tax Registration Form. The system extracts personal and business information from citizenship certificates, business registration documents, and naksha pass certificates using Google Gemini Vision API and Tesseract OCR, then automatically fills the extracted data into the appropriate form fields and generates print-ready PDF documents. Users can create profiles to save their information for future form submissions, and an admin panel allows office staff to manage user records. 

However, the system has certain limitations: it requires 
a JSON template configuration file for each new form type, which means adding support for additional government forms requires manual template creation with field coordinate measurements. The accuracy of data extraction depends on the quality and clarity of uploaded document images blurred, damaged, or poorly scanned documents may result in extraction errors that require manual correction. The system also requires an active internet connection to use the Gemini AI service, though it automatically falls back to offline OCR when the internet is unavailable. Currently, the system is optimized for Nepali government forms with Devanagari script and may not perform well with forms in other languages or significantly different layouts. Additionally, while the system validates common data formats like phone numbers and email addresses, it cannot verify the authenticity of documents or detect fraudulent information it assumes all uploaded documents are genuine and issued by authorized government bodies.

\chapter{BACKGROUND STUDY AND LITERATURE REVIEW}
\section{Background Study}
The Auto Form Fill-Up Application relies on several core technologies working 
together. Optical Character Recognition (OCR) is a technology that converts text from images into machine-readable format Tesseract OCR serves as the fallback extraction mechanism in this system when internet connectivity is unavailable. Artificial Intelligence for document understanding goes beyond simple text recognition by semantically interpreting document content Google Gemini Vision API analyzes uploaded citizenship certificates and business documents, understanding context and field relationships rather than just extracting raw text. PDF generation involves programmatically creating documents with precise text placement and formatting ReportLab library handles this by rendering extracted data onto government form templates with accurate Devanagari font support. Database Management Systems provide structured storage and retrieval of information SQLAlchemy ORM manages user profiles and form submission records in either SQLite or MySQL databases. Form automation refers to the process of automatically populating form fields with extracted data this project uses JSON-based templates that map source document fields to target form coordinates. Finally, Devanagari Script Processing ensures proper handling of Nepali Unicode characters throughout the system, from extraction through PDF generation, using NotoSans Devanagari fonts for accurate rendering of official government forms. 

\section{Literature Review}
Several researchers have explored automated document processing and form filling systems. Smith \cite{smith2007} presented Tesseract OCR engine architecture, demonstrating its effectiveness in recognizing text across multiple languages including Devanagari script, though accuracy depends significantly on image quality and pre-processing techniques. Antonacopoulos and colleagues \cite{antonacopoulos2003} developed methodologies for evaluating document layout analysis systems, emphasizing the importance of understanding form structure before data extraction; this research informed the template-based approach used in this project.

Denk and Reisswig \cite{denk2020} introduced BERTgrid for contextualized document representation, showing that AI models can understand relationships between fields rather than treating documents as isolated text fragments. This concept directly influenced the decision to use Gemini Vision API for semantic field mapping. Dhakal \cite{dhakal2019} work on one-shot template matching for automatic document data capture demonstrated that predefined templates significantly improve extraction accuracy compared to fully dynamic approaches; this validation supported the JSON template methodology implemented in this system.

In the context of Nepal, Bhattarai and Adhikari \cite{bhattarai2019} analyzed e-governance initiatives, identifying manual form processing as a major bottleneck in government service delivery. Their research highlighted the need for practical automation solutions that require minimal infrastructure and technical expertise, which guided the design decisions for making this application accessible to sub-metropolitan office staff with limited technical backgrounds.

\chapter{SYSTEM ANALYSIS AND DESIGN}
\section{System Analysis}
The system analysis phase focuses on understanding the requirements, feasibility, and structure of the Auto Form Fill-Up Application. This analysis examines the functionality needed to automate government form processing, the technical and operational constraints, and the data flow throughout the system. The application is designed as a desktop-based solution using Streamlit for the user interface, Python for backend processing, and SQLAlchemy for database management. The system supports document upload, AI-powered data extraction, manual data review and editing, and PDF generation with proper Devanagari rendering for Nepali government forms.

\subsection{Requirement Analysis}
\subsubsection{Functional Requirements}
The functional requirements define the core features and capabilities that the Auto Form Fill-Up Application must provide to users. These requirements were identified through observation during internship at municipal offices and analysis of the manual form-filling workflow currently in use.
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbullet, leftmargin=*, nosep, itemsep=2pt, topsep=2pt]
\item\textbf{User Registration and Authentication:} The system allows users to create accounts   using their email address and password. Password authentication ensures that user data remains private and accessible only to the account owner. The system stores hashed passwords for security.

\item\textbf{Document Upload:} Users can upload multiple document images simultaneously, including citizenship certificates, business registration papers, and naksha pass documents. Supported formats include JPEG, PNG, and TIFF files.

\item\textbf{Intelligent Data Extraction:} The system extracts text and field data from uploaded documents using Google Gemini Vision API as the primary extraction engine. When internet connectivity is unavailable or API limits are reached, the system automatically falls back to Tesseract OCR for local text extraction.

\item\textbf{Template-Based Form Mapping:} The application uses JSON configuration files that define field locations, types, and validation rules for each government form type. Currently supports Land and Property Tax Form (Sampati Kar) and Business Tax Registration Form.

\item\textbf{Data Review and Editing:} After extraction, users can review all extracted fields, view confidence scores indicating extraction accuracy, and manually edit any incorrect or missing information before generating the final PDF.

\item\textbf{Data Validation:} The system validates critical fields including Nepali phone numbers (must be 10 digits starting with 9, second digit 7 or 8), email addresses (must contain @ and domain), and required field completeness.

\item\textbf{User Profile Management:} Users can save their personal information to their profile, enabling automatic form filling for future submissions without re-entering the same data repeatedly.

\item\textbf{PDF Generation:} The system generates print-ready PDF documents with extracted data accurately positioned in the correct fields, using NotoSans Devanagari font for proper Nepali Unicode rendering.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{Non-Functional Requirements}
It specify the quality attributes and performance 
characteristics that the system must maintain:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbullet, leftmargin=*, nosep, itemsep=2pt, topsep=2pt]
\item\textbf{Performance:} The system should complete data extraction within 15-30 seconds for standard-quality citizenship certificate images using Gemini API. OCR fallback processing should complete within 5-10 seconds per image. 
\item\textbf{Accuracy:} Gemini-based extraction should achieve minimum 85 percent accuracy for clear, high-quality document images. OCR fallback should achieve minimum 70 percent accuracy. The system must clearly indicate confidence levels for each extracted field to help users identify fields requiring manual review.

 \item\textbf{Usability:} The interface must be intuitive enough for government office staff with minimal computer literacy to operate effectively. All primary functions (upload, extract, review, generate) should be accessible within 2-3 clicks. Error messages must be clear and actionable in both English and Nepali context.

\item\textbf{Reliability:} The system should maintain 95 percent uptime during office hours. The automatic fallback from Gemini to OCR must activate seamlessly without user intervention when API failures occur.

\item\textbf{Security:} User passwords must be hashed using bcrypt with proper salting. The system must prevent SQL injection through parameterized queries. User data must remain isolated - users can only access their own profiles and submissions.

\item\textbf{Maintainability:} The codebase follows modular architecture with clear separation between extraction, validation, PDF generation, and database operations. Each module should be independently testable and updatable.

\item\textbf{Scalability:} The system should support adding new form templates by simply creating new JSON configuration files without modifying application code.

\item\textbf{Compatibility:} The application must run on standard office computers running Windows 10 or later with minimum 4GB RAM and Python 3.11+. Database should support both MySQL for networked deployments and SQLite for standalone operation.
\end{enumerate}
\subsection{Feasibility Analysis}
\subsubsection{Technical Feasibility}
The Auto Form Fill-Up Application is technically feasible based on the availability of mature, well-documented technologies. Python 3.11 provides a robust foundation with extensive library support for image processing, database operations, and PDF generation. Google Gemini Vision API offers commercial-grade document understanding capabilities with RESTful API access, while Tesseract OCR provides reliable offline text extraction with Nepali language support. Streamlit framework enables rapid development of interactive web interfaces without requiring extensive frontend development expertise. SQLAlchemy ORM abstracts database operations, allowing the system to work with both MySQL and SQLite without code changes. ReportLab library successfully handles PDF generation with Unicode font embedding for Devanagari script. All required technologies are open-source or offer free tiers suitable for government office deployment, and comprehensive documentation exists for each component. The development team has access to necessary development tools including Python IDEs, version control.


\subsubsection{Operational Feasibility}
The system demonstrates strong operational feasibility as it directly addresses real pain points observed during internship at municipal offices. Current manual processes require 15-30 minutes per citizen for form filling, data transcription, and verification. The automated system reduces this to 3-5 minutes including document upload, extraction, review, and PDF generation - a 70-80 percent time reduction. Office staff require minimal training as the Streamlit interface follows familiar web application patterns with clear visual feedback at each step. The system maintains human oversight by requiring users to review and confirm extracted data before PDF generation, addressing staff concerns about automation accuracy and accountability. The dual extraction approach (Gemini with OCR fallback) ensures operation continues even during internet outages, maintaining workflow continuity. User profile functionality directly addresses the common scenario where the same citizens return for different services, eliminating redundant data entry. The system integrates into existing workflows without requiring changes to official form templates or government procedures - it simply automates the filling process while maintaining the same official PDF outputs that government offices currently use.

\subsubsection{Economic Feasibility}

Google Gemini API offers a free tier with 60 requests per minute, sufficient for typical municipal office volumes of 20-50 forms daily. If usage exceeds free tier limits, paid API access costs approximately Rs10-20 per image, resulting in monthly costs under RS600-RS700 for an office processing 100 forms daily. The system runs on existing office computers without requiring specialized hardware - minimum requirements of 
4GB RAM and dual-core processor are met by standard government office equipment purchased in the last 5 years. SQLite database option eliminates server costs for small offices, while MySQL deployment for larger offices uses free open-source database software. Staff time savings provide immediate return on investment: reducing processing time from 30 minutes to 5 minutes per form saves 25 minutes of staff time, valued at approximately NPR 50-100 per form based on average clerical wages. An office processing 50 forms daily saves 20+ staff hours per week, equivalent to half a full-time position. Initial development and deployment costs are minimal, primarily consisting of developer time for customization and staff training. The system pays for itself within the first month of operation through labor cost savings alone.

\subsection{Interface Design}
The user interface design prioritizes simplicity and clarity, following a 
step-by-step workflow that guides users through the form-filling process. The 
Streamlit framework provides the foundation with its clean, responsive layout and automatic state management.

The main interface consists of five sequential sections presented as numbered cards:

Step 1 - Template Selection: A dropdown menu displays available form types with Nepali names. The selection determines which 
JSON template will be used for field mapping.

Step 2 - Document Upload: A drag-and-drop file uploader accepts multiple images simultaneously. Uploaded images display as thumbnails in a responsive grid layout, providing immediate visual confirmation of successful upload.

Step 3 - Data Extraction: A prominent "Extract with Gemini" button initiates 
processing. A progress bar with status text provides real-time feedback ("Analyzing documents...", "Extraction completed"). If Gemini fails, an automatic OCR fallback activates with appropriate messaging to the user.

Step 4 - Review and Edit: Extracted data displays in a two-column form layout with field labels in both Nepali and English. Each field shows a confidence indicator (green circle for high confidence, yellow for medium, red for low) helping users identify fields needing careful review. Text input boxes allow inline editing of any field. A "Load My Saved Data" button enables one-click autofill from user profile. Input validation provides immediate feedback for phone numbers and emails.

Step 5 - PDF Generation: A "Generate PDF" button creates the filled form. A 
checkbox option "Save data to my profile" lets users update their stored information. Upon successful generation, a download button appears with the PDF file name.

The sidebar provides persistent access to user profile management and admin panel (for authorized users). Profile features include account creation, login, and logout with clear visual indication of login status. The admin panel displays user statistics and management functions in collapsible sections.

Color scheme follows standard web conventions: blue for primary actions, green for success states, yellow for warnings, and red for errors. The interface maintains sufficient contrast for readability and uses 12pt font size for body text with larger headings for section titles.

Error messages appear inline near the relevant field or action button, using clear language explaining what went wrong and how to fix it ("Phone must be 10 digits starting with 9").

\section{Implementation}
The Auto Form Fill-Up Application was implemented using Python 3.11 as the primary programming language with a modular architecture separating concerns across different components. The development followed an iterative approach, beginning with core extraction functionality, then adding validation and PDF generation, and finally implementing the user interface and database integration. The entire codebase is organized into distinct modules handling extraction, form filling, PDF generation, database operations, and user interface presentation.


\subsection{Tools Used}
The implementation utilized Streamlit for building the interactive web interface, Google Gemini Vision API and Tesseract OCR for document text extraction, ReportLab for PDF generation with Devanagari font support, SQLAlchemy ORM for database abstraction supporting both MySQL and SQLite, OpenCV and Pillow for image processing, and Python-dotenv for environment configuration management. Version control was maintained using Git, and the development environment included VS Code as the primary 
code editor with Python virtual environment for dependency isolation.

\subsection{Implementation Details of Modules}
The system is organized into the following functional modules:
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbullet, leftmargin=*, nosep, itemsep=2pt, topsep=2pt]

\item \textbf{Gemini Extractor Module (app/gemini/extractor.py):}
Interfaces with Google Gemini Vision API to perform semantic extraction of text and field data from uploaded document images using AI-powered document understanding.

\item \textbf{OCR Fallback Module (app/ocr/extractor.py):}
Implements Tesseract-based optical character recognition as a backup extraction mechanism when internet connectivity is unavailable or API limits are exceeded.

\item \textbf{Form Filler Module (app/filler/form-filler.py):}
Normalizes extracted data by validating formats, converting Nepali numerals to Arabic digits, and mapping source fields to target form fields according to template specifications.

\item \textbf{PDF Generator Module (app/printer/pdf-generator.py):}
Creates filled PDF documents by overlaying extracted text onto government form templates with precise coordinate positioning and proper Devanagari Unicode rendering.

\item \textbf{Database Module (app/db/):}
Manages data persistence using SQLAlchemy ORM, including models for user profiles and form submissions, connection handling with MySQL/SQLite, and session management for database transactions.

\item \textbf{Template Utilities Module (app/utils/templates.py):}
Loads and parses JSON template configuration files that define form field locations, types, validation rules, and metadata for supported government forms.

\item \textbf{User Interface Module (app/frontend/ui.py):}
Implements the Streamlit-based web interface providing document upload functionality, extraction progress tracking, data review and editing capabilities, PDF generation controls, user profile management, and administrative user management panel.

\item \textbf{Validation Module (app/utils/validators.py):}
Enforces data integrity by validating Nepali phone number formats, email address patterns, and required field completeness before allowing form submission and PDF generation.

\end{enumerate}

\chapter{CONCLUSION AND FUTURE RECOMMENDATIONS}

\section{Lessons Learned / Outcome}
During the development of this project, several valuable lessons emerged regarding real-world software development and working with AI technologies. The most significant challenge was handling the unpredictability of AI extraction—Gemini Vision API occasionally timed out or produced inconsistent results, which emphasized the importance of implementing robust fallback mechanisms and error handling. Managing Devanagari Unicode text throughout the system required careful attention to character encoding and font rendering, particularly when generating PDFs that matched official government templates precisely.

The project successfully demonstrated that automation can significantly reduce manual workload in government offices while maintaining accuracy. Testing with real citizenship certificates and business documents showed average extraction accuracy of 85-90 percent with Gemini AI and 70-75 percent with OCR fallback. The user profile feature proved particularly valuable, reducing repeat data entry time by approximately 80 percent for returning users. This practical experience highlighted the importance of designing systems that balance automation with human oversight, allowing staff to review and correct extracted data before final submission.


\section{Conclusion}
This project successfully developed a functional Auto Form Fill-Up Application that addresses real challenges faced by sub-metropolitan offices in Nepal. The system effectively automates the extraction of information from citizenship certificates and business documents, intelligently maps this data to government form templates, and generates accurate PDF outputs with proper Devanagari rendering. The dual extraction approach using Gemini AI with OCR fallback ensures reliability even under 
varying network conditions.

The application demonstrates the feasibility of modernizing government form processing through practical technology implementation. While designed as an academic prototype focusing on two specific forms, the template-based architecture provides a foundation for expanding to additional government services. This project validates that even resource-constrained government offices can benefit from automation tools that require minimal infrastructure and technical expertise to operate effectively.
\section{Future Recommendations}
The Auto Form Fill-Up Application can be enhanced in several directions to increase its utility and coverage. Based on lessons learned during development and feedback from testing, the following improvements are recommended for future iterations.
\subsubsection{Immediate Enhancements:}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbullet, leftmargin=*, nosep, itemsep=2pt, topsep=2pt]
\item\textbf{Mobile Application Development: }Create Android/iOS versions allowing citizens to fill forms directly from their smartphones using phone cameras for document capture.

\item\textbf{Expanded Form Support:} Add templates for additional government forms including vehicle registration, marriage certificates, and land ownership transfer documents.

\item\textbf{ Batch Processing Mode:} Implement functionality to process multiple forms simultaneously for offices handling high volumes during peak periods.

\item\textbf{ Offline Mode Optimization:} Enhance OCR accuracy with Nepali-language-specific training  data to improve extraction quality when internet connectivity is unavailable.

\item\textbf{ Digital Signature Integration:} Add support for digital signatures to enable completely paperless form submission and verification.

\subsubsection{Long-term Research Directions:}
\item\textbf{ Dynamic Form Detection:} Develop computer vision models that can automatically understand new form structures without requiring manual template creation, using deep learning for layout analysis.

\item\textbf{Semantic Field Matching with NLP:} Implement natural language processing to intelligently match source document fields to target forms even when terminology differs, handling synonyms and contextual variations.

\item\textbf{Blockchain-based Verification:} Integrate blockchain technology for tamper-proof record keeping and verification of submitted forms, enhancing security and trust in digital  government services.
\end{enumerate}
